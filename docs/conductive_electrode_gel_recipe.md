# Conductive Electrode Gel Recipe

## Purpose

This gel is intended for use between the skin and stimulation or sensing electrodes to:

- Improve electrical contact between electrode and skin
- Reduce electrode-skin impedance
- Remain hydrated longer than a simple salt-water gel
- Provide a stable, easy-to-apply gel consistency

**Warning:** This recipe contains **2% NaCl**. Some people may experience skin irritation, especially during prolonged use or electrical stimulation. If irritation occurs, discontinue use and consider reducing the salt concentration to approximately **0.9–1.0% NaCl**.

## Ingredients (100 g batch)

- 77 g distilled water
- 20 g glycerin
- 2 g sodium chloride (NaCl, table salt)
- 1 g xanthan gum

## Equipment

- Small mixing container
- Digital scale
- Whisk, milk frother, or immersion blender
- Storage container with lid

## Mixing Instructions

### Method 1 (recommended)

1. Weigh 77 g distilled water into a mixing container.
2. Add 2 g NaCl and stir until completely dissolved.
3. Add 20 g glycerin and mix thoroughly.
4. In a separate small cup, make a smooth slurry by mixing the 1 g xanthan gum with a small portion of the glycerin mixture.
5. Slowly add the xanthan slurry back into the main mixture while stirring vigorously.
6. Continue stirring for 1–2 minutes until evenly dispersed.
7. Allow the gel to rest for 15–60 minutes so the xanthan gum can fully hydrate.
8. Stir once more before use.

### Alternative Method

1. Dissolve the NaCl in the water.
2. Add the glycerin and mix.
3. Slowly sprinkle the xanthan gum into the liquid while stirring continuously.
4. Mix thoroughly and allow to hydrate for 15–60 minutes.

## Notes

- No heating is required.
- If the gel is too thick, add a small amount of distilled water.
- If the gel is too thin, add a little more xanthan gum.
- Store in a sealed container in the refrigerator.
- This recipe contains **no preservative**. Discard and make a fresh batch after
  **2 weeks**, or immediately if the gel develops odor, discoloration, mold, or
  any visible contamination.
- For longer shelf life, prepare small batches rather than storing large quantities.
